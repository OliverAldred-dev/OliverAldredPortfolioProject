Dead by Daylight Inspired Locker – Environment Prop

Concept Development

At the beginning of this project, I explored the themes and gameplay mechanics of Dead by Daylight. To help organize my ideas, I created a mind map that broke down the main elements of the game, such as stealth, survival, suffering, sacrifice, and attack. This helped me better understand the atmosphere of the game and think about how a simple model can reflect those themes. One gameplay mechanic that stood out to me was the locker, which survivors often use to hide from the killer. Lockers represent a moment of tension in the game, where players are vulnerable but trying to stay hidden. Because of this, I chose to model a locker as the focus of the project, as it felt like a strong model that captures the suspense in the game.

Concept Development

The locker was created in Blender using a low-poly style that would be suitable for use in a game environment. I started with a basic cube and scaled it to show the overall proportions of the locker. From there, I used tools such as extrude and inset faces to build up the main features of the model. This includes the double locker doors, vent panels, handles, and small panel details that give the asset more depth. Throughout the modeling process, I tried to keep the geometry as simple as possible by using simple loop cuts and basic shapes. This helped keep the model more optimized while still adding enough detail to make it recognizable as a locker. I also regularly checked the shadows and proportions to make sure the model looked believable and would be presented clearly if placed inside a game environment.

Lighting and Scene Setup

After completing the model, I set up a simple scene to experiment with lighting and atmosphere, inspired by the horror tone of Dead by Daylight. I placed the locker against a curved backdrop so that the lighting would fall more naturally across the scene. I then added a strong red spotlight as the main light source. Red lighting is often used in horror games to suggest danger, tension, or violence, so it helped reinforce the mood I was trying to create. The lighting setup focused on:
  • Creating strong contrast between light and shadow
  • Drawing attention to the locker as the main subject
  • Supporting the dark and tense atmosphere
Keeping most of the environment dark helped contain the model and make it feel more dramatic.

Final Presentation

The final render shows the locker in a small scene so that the shape, lighting, and mood remain as the focus of the scene. The red light highlights the front of the locker while the rest of the environment fades into darkness, helping create a sense of tension and uncertainty. Through this project, I improved my skills in:
  • 3D modeling
  • Using lighting to create atmosphere
  • Low-poly asset creation for games
  • Translating gameplay ideas into visual design
Overall, this project allowed me to explore how a simple prop can still convey the themes and mood of a game, and I enjoyed the process of bringing a piece of the Dead by Daylight world to life through 3D modeling and lighting.

 

Self-Evaluation

Overall, I am happy with how this project turned out. I think the locker model successfully captures the themes of Dead by Daylight while also being a simple and effective 3D asset. The lighting setup helped create a strong atmosphere that supports the mood of the game. Some challenges I faced included ensuring the model was optimized for performance while maintaining its visual appeal. This is because the model was quite hard to integrate the handles without using too many poly's. I would develop the idea further by addin a more complex texture that would represent the more worn and rusty feeling on the locker. I would also consider adding more detailed lighting effects to enhance the atmospheric impact.
